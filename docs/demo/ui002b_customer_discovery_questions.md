# UI-002B Customer Discovery Questions

Use these questions after or before the UI-002B manual claimant-flow demo.

## Current Claims Process

- Walk me through a typical travel claim from intake to adjuster decision.
- Which claim types take the most adjuster time?
- Where do claims usually wait the longest?
- How many travel claims per month are in the ₪5,000–₪15,000 range?
- What percentage of these claims are straightforward, suspicious, or missing
  information?

## Missing Information Loop

- How often do adjusters request missing documents or clarifications?
- How is that request sent today?
- Who sends the request: adjuster, service team, broker, or another role?
- What percentage of claims get delayed because claimant responses are
  incomplete?
- How many back-and-forth cycles are typical before the claim can move forward?
- Which missing documents or clarifications are most common?
- What makes a claimant response unusable for the adjuster?

## Fraud / Inflation Triage

- Which fraud or inflation signals are most useful to adjusters today?
- What kinds of inconsistencies do adjusters manually compare across documents?
- Which signals create too many false positives?
- What evidence must be visible before an adjuster trusts a recommendation?
- Where should Spectix stop and leave judgment to the adjuster?

## Adjuster Workload

- How many claims does one adjuster handle per day or week?
- Which review steps are repetitive enough that software help would matter?
- How much time is spent preparing a brief before making a decision?
- How much time is spent chasing claimant information?
- What would make this workflow clearly faster for an adjuster?

## Existing Tools

- What claim management system is used today?
- Where are documents stored?
- How do adjusters communicate with claimants?
- Is there an existing rules engine or fraud tool?
- What export, API, or integration would be needed for a pilot?

## Pilot Readiness

- Who would approve a pilot?
- What would you need to see to sign an LOI?
- Which team would provide pilot claims or sample files?
- What pilot size would be meaningful?
- What success metric would justify continuing after a pilot?

## Security / Procurement Blockers

- What data/security condition would block a pilot?
- Is data residency in Israel mandatory for pilot, production, or both?
- Is a DPIA required before pilot?
- What procurement/security review is required for a vendor handling claim
  files?
- Are synthetic or anonymized files acceptable for early evaluation?

## Budget / Value

- What is the current cost of manually handling missing-information loops?
- What cost or time saving would be meaningful enough to buy?
- Who owns the budget for claims operations tools?
- Is value measured by adjuster productivity, loss reduction, fraud detection,
  cycle time, or customer experience?
- What pricing model would be easiest to approve for a pilot?

## LOI Qualification

- If the demo matches the workflow, what is the next step?
- Who else must see this before an LOI?
- What legal/security language must be included in an LOI?
- What timeline would be realistic for a pilot decision?
- What would make you say no after this conversation?
